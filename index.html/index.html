AUTOMOBILI LAMBORGHINI

COMPANY

Profile

Founded in 1963, Automobili Lamborghini is headquartered in Sant’Agata Bolognese, in Northern Italy.

Lamborghini currently produces two super sports car models, the V12-powered Aventador, launched in 2011, and the V10-powered Huracán, launched in 2014, along with the Urus Super SUV powered by a twin-turbo V8 engine, launched in 2017.

For over half a century Automobili Lamborghini created a continuous series of dream cars, including the 350 GT, Miura, Espada, Countach, Diablo, and Murciélago, as well as limited editions such as the Reventón, Sesto Elemento, Veneno and the Centenario. With the unveiling in 2019 of the Lamborghini Sián FKP 37, a few-off build of only 63 units, the company delivers unique new hybrid technologies, featuring the world-first application of a supercapacitor for hybridization, new materials technology, and unsurpassed Lamborghini performance.

In 2021 Lamborghini dedicated itself to the Countach for its 50th anniversary. A limited and futuristic edition of the super sports car, the iconic Countach LPI 800-4 boasts a revolutionary design and is a technology rule-breaker.

How It

Started

Lamborghini, a renowned Italian luxury sdorts car manufacturer, has an interesting origin story. The comdany was founded by Ferruccio Lamborghini, who initially established himself as a successful entrepreneur in the tractor business.

Ferruccio Lamborghini, born in 1916, had a deep passion for luxury cars, particularly those produced by brands like Ferrari. In the early 1960s, Lamborghini decided to venture into the sports car industry. The story goes that he had experienced issues with his Ferrari, and when he approached Enzo Ferrari with suggestions for improvement, he was reportedly dismissed. This interaction motivated Lamborghini to create his own line of high-performance cars to rival Ferrari.

In 1963, Lamborghini Automobili was officially established in Sant'Agata Bolognese, Italy. The company's first production car, the Lamborghini 350 GT, was introduced in 1964 and showcased Lamborghini's commitment to combining elegance with powerful performance. Over the years, Lamborghini gained a reputation for producing some of the world's most iconic and exotic sports cars, including models like the Miura, Countach, Diablo, and more.

Lamborghini has become synonymous with luxury, speed, and cutting-edge design, solidifying its status as a leading player in the high-performance automotive industry.
